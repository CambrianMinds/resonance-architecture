## The Three Mechanisms

### Mechanism 1: Vibrational Surface Fitting

**Principle**: Sustained vibration at contact surfaces causes preferential abrasion at high-contact points, automatically optimizing toward maximum surface contact.

**Evidence**:
- Pillowed/undulating faces on megalithic blocks (wear pattern)
- Interior joint geometry that changes direction (ruled out insertion)
- Wavy joint lines (natural result of vibrational fitting)
- Consistent with Chris Dunn's ultrasonic machining analysis

**Key Insight** (from Dunn):
> "Quartz crystals... are responsive to the influence of vibration in the ultrasonic ranges and can be induced to vibrate at high frequency... Instead of resisting the cutting action, the quartz would be induced to respond and vibrate in sympathy with the high frequency waves and amplify the abrasive action."

The quartz in granite becomes an **active participant** in the shaping process, not an obstacle.

**Nub Function**: Anchor points for vibrational tools/transducers. Their placement on lower courses (where stone meets already-placed blocks) supports this function. They remain because removal would damage the precision-fitted surface.

### Laboratory Tests
1. **Friction reduction under vibration**: Measure coefficient of friction between stone surfaces with and without applied vibration at various frequencies
2. **Surface fitting under vibration**: Allow two rough stone surfaces to abrade under sustained vibration; measure contact area over time
3. **Piezoelectric output**: Measure electrical output from granite under sustained acoustic excitation
4. **Quartz response**: Test differential wear rates in granite under ultrasonic vs. conventional abrasion
