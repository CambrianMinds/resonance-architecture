## 1. The 110 Hz Phenomenon

### UCLA Brain Study (Cook et al., 2008)

**Source:** "Ancient Architectural Acoustic Resonance Patterns and Regional Brain Activity" - Published in Time and Mind

**Methodology:** Volunteers monitored by EEG while exposed to different resonance frequencies through headphones.

**Key Finding:** At 110 Hz, the patterns of activity over the prefrontal cortex **abruptly shifted**, resulting in:
- Relative **deactivation of the language center**
- Temporary shifting from **left to right-sided dominance**
- Right hemisphere associated with **emotional processing, spatial reasoning, creativity**

**Significance:** The frequency found in ancient chambers (Malta, Ireland, Egypt) corresponds to measurable neurological effects. This isn't mysticism—it's neuroscience.

### University of Trieste Study (Debertolis et al., 2014)

**Source:** SBRG Clinical Neurophysiology Unit testing

**Key Finding:** Each individual has their own "private pitch" between 70-130 Hz at which the brain responds.
- Volunteers with **frontal lobe prevalence** received "ideas and thoughts similar to meditation"
- Volunteers with **occipital lobe prevalence** visualized images

**Conclusion:** "Ancient populations were able to obtain different states of consciousness without the use of drugs or other chemical substances."

## 4. Piezoelectric Effects in Stone

### Earthquake Lights Research

**Researchers:** Professor Freund, San Jose University
**Published:** IEEE Spectrum, multiple peer-reviewed journals

**Data Compiled:** 65 sites worldwide where earthquake lights were observed

**Mechanism Confirmed:**
1. Stress on granite activates piezoelectric charge in quartz crystals
2. When voltage is high enough, it ionizes surrounding air
3. Ionized air produces visible light ("earthquake lights")

**Relevance:** Massive granite structures under compressive stress (like the Great Pyramid's "relieving chambers") would exhibit piezoelectric effects.

### Granite Under Stress Study (Kato, Mitsui, Yanagidani, 2010)

**Published:** Earth, Planets and Space

**Experiment:** Granite slab stressed with high pressure until light began to glow on surface.

**Finding:** Granite under stress builds voltage potential on its surface. If voltage is high enough, it ionizes the air, creating visible glow.

**Implications:** Ancient underground chambers carved in granite, with no evidence of soot, may have been illuminated by **stressed stone** rather than fire.
