## 1. Wally Wallington - Mechanical Advantage Demonstrations

### Background
- **Who**: Wally Wallington, retired construction worker from Michigan
- **What**: Single-person movement and lifting of multi-ton stone blocks
- **Method**: Leverage, pivoting, counterweights - pure mechanical advantage

### Demonstrations
- Lifted 22,000 lb (10-ton) pillar single-handedly
- Moved 300+ feet barn by himself
- Constructing full-scale Stonehenge replica using only hand tools

### Key Techniques
1. **Pivoting**: Using small fulcrum points to rotate massive stones
2. **Walking**: Rocking stones side-to-side creates forward motion
3. **Counterweighting**: Balance points allow massive weights to be controlled
4. **Incremental raising**: Small steps accumulate to large lifts

### Relevance to Hypothesis
- Demonstrates that "impossible" feats are possible with correct technique
- Shows ancient builders may have had knowledge we've forgotten
- Mechanical advantage works, but requires specific understanding
- **Does not address**: Precision fitting, surface finishing, acoustic methods

### Sources
- YouTube channel: wallywallington
- Wikipedia: Wally Wallington
- Various documentary appearances

## 6. Acoustic Levitation Demonstrations

### Modern Laboratory Demos
- Ultrasonic transducers create standing waves
- Small objects suspended in pressure nodes
- Currently limited to small objects (<half wavelength)

### DIY Acoustic Levitation
- Instructables guide available
- Uses ultrasonic transducers (40 kHz typical)
- Can levitate styrofoam, water droplets, small objects

### Limitations
- Power requirements scale dramatically with object size
- Currently impractical for large objects
- But proves the physics works

### Relevance
- Confirms sound can produce force on objects
- Current scaling is limiting factor, not physics
- Ancient methods may have used different approach (friction reduction vs. true levitation)

## 7. Proposed Experiments (Not Yet Conducted)

### Friction Reduction Under Vibration
**Setup**: 
- Two stone surfaces
- Vibration source (speaker, mechanical oscillator)
- Force measurement (load cell, spring scale)

**Protocol**:
1. Measure static friction coefficient without vibration
2. Apply vibration at various frequencies and amplitudes
3. Measure dynamic friction coefficient
4. Document frequency/amplitude relationship

**Predicted outcome**: Significant friction reduction at certain frequencies

---

### Vibrational Surface Fitting
**Setup**:
- Two rough stone surfaces
- Sustained vibration source
- Abrasive medium (sand, water)

**Protocol**:
1. Measure initial contact area (dye transfer method)
2. Apply sustained vibration with abrasive
3. Measure contact area at intervals
4. Document surface evolution

**Predicted outcome**: Contact area increases over time; pillowed appearance develops

---

### Nub Function Testing
**Setup**:
- Stone block with manufactured "nub"
- Vibrational tool (could be simple speaker/transducer)
- Various attachment methods

**Protocol**:
1. Attempt to couple vibration to stone without nub
2. Attempt to couple vibration using nub as anchor
3. Measure vibration transmission efficiency

**Predicted outcome**: Nub provides more efficient coupling for sustained vibration

---

### Piezoelectric Output Measurement
**Setup**:
- Granite sample with known quartz content
- Acoustic excitation source
- High-impedance voltmeter or oscilloscope

**Protocol**:
1. Excite granite with sustained acoustic vibration
2. Measure electrical output at various points
3. Vary frequency, measure response
4. Document resonance effects

**Predicted outcome**: Measurable piezoelectric output, increased at resonant frequencies
