## Site Overview

### Location
- Sacred Valley, Peru
- ~60 km northwest of Cusco
- Elevation: ~2,792 meters (9,160 ft)
- On the banks of the Urubamba River

### Official Attribution
- Inca construction, 15th century AD
- Built during reign of Pachacuti
- Combined military, religious, and agricultural functions

### The Problem
Multiple features suggest **earlier construction** or **unknown technology**:
- Precision exceeding known Inca capabilities
- Massive stone transport from distant quarry
- Distinctive techniques different from demonstrably Inca work

## The Wall of Six Monoliths

### Physical Description
- Six massive rose-colored porphyry/rhyolite blocks
- Each estimated 50+ tons
- Standing approximately 4.6 meters (15 feet) high
- Arranged vertically side by side
- **Separated by thin stone spacers** (key feature)

### The Spacers
Between each massive monolith are **thin vertical slabs** of stone:
- Function unknown
- Too thin to be structural
- Perfectly fitted to both adjacent monoliths
- May have acoustic or vibrational significance

**Hypothesis**: Spacers may have served as:
- Resonance dampeners
- Frequency tuners
- Acoustic separation between resonant masses
- Evidence of vibrational considerations in design

### Surface Features
- Pillowed/cushioned faces (same signature as Cusco walls)
- Precision fitting despite immense scale
- No mortar
- Nubs/bosses visible on some surfaces

## The Quarry Problem

### Cachicata Quarry
- Located across the Urubamba Valley
- Approximately 6 km (4 miles) from Ollantaytambo
- Elevation difference: quarry is HIGHER than construction site
- Route requires: descending mountain, crossing river, ascending to temple

### The "Tired Stones" (Piedras Cansadas)
Massive partially-worked stones abandoned along the route:
- Scattered between quarry and temple
- Some show preparation marks
- Local legend calls them "tired stones"
- Suggests interrupted transport process

**Question**: Why were they abandoned? 
- Conventional: Workers died, project stopped, random failures
- Alternative: Transport method failed at specific locations

**If vibrational transport**:
- Stones may have "stopped" where resonance conditions failed
- Terrain features may have disrupted vibrational transmission
- Specific locations might correlate with acoustic anomalies

### Transport Logistics
Conventional explanation requires:
- Massive labor force
- Ramps, rollers, ropes
- River crossing with 50-ton stones
- Uphill transport to temple platform

**No clear evidence** of:
- Ramp structures sufficient for this weight
- River crossing infrastructure
- Rope/roller marks on stones
- Construction camps for required labor force

## Construction Anomalies

### Precision Inconsistency
The site shows **two distinct construction styles**:

**Style 1 - Megalithic/Precision**:
- Massive blocks
- Perfect fitting
- Pillowed surfaces
- Thin spacers
- No visible tool marks
- **Attributed by some to pre-Inca civilization**

**Style 2 - Standard Inca**:
- Smaller stones
- Good but not exceptional fitting
- Visible tool marks
- More rustic construction
- Clearly matches known Inca techniques

**Implication**: The Inca may have **inherited** and **built upon** an earlier structure using techniques they didn't fully understand.

### The Unfinished State
The Temple of the Sun appears **intentionally unfinished**:
- Or: Construction was interrupted
- Or: It was completed for a purpose we don't recognize

The "unfinished" interpretation assumes we understand what "finished" means for this structure. If it was a resonant machine rather than a building, our expectations may be wrong.

### Observations
- Nubs visible on many stones at site
- Variable sizes and placements
- Some removed (smooth scars visible)

### Distribution Pattern (To Be Mapped)
